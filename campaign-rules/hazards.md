# Hazards

Hazards are environmental threats that aren't monsters: traps, unstable structures, toxic atmospheres, extreme weather, and other conditions that damage or impede the party independent of combat.

---

## Traps

Most traps are detected through player attention rather than die rolls: describing what you look for, using the 10ft pole, checking before you step. When detection depends on luck or speed, use the standard task roll (2-in-6 default; Delvers use their skill rating).

Triggered traps deal damage or apply conditions. Use the same damage die scale as monsters of equivalent tier.

---

## Environmental Threats

Treat environmental damage (falling, drowning, fire, extreme cold) the same as monster damage: apply a damage die appropriate to the severity, allow a save to reduce or avoid where applicable.

| Severity | Damage |
|----------|--------|
| Minor (short fall, brief flame) | d4 |
| Moderate (significant fall, sustained exposure) | d6 |
| Severe (great height, hostile environment) | d8-d10 |
| Lethal (instant-kill scenarios) | Save or defeated |

---

## Light and Darkness

Track light sources. A torch lasts 6 dungeon turns. A lantern lasts longer but consumes oil. Running out of light is not an automatic death sentence but it is a serious problem: most enemies can navigate darkness better than humans can.
