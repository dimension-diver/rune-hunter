# Encounters

## Wandering Encounters

Roll for wandering encounters after every 3 dungeon turns (2-in-6 chance). A result doesn't always mean a fight: roll a reaction to determine disposition.

---

## Surprise

2-in-6 chance for either side to be surprised at the start of an encounter. Surprised side loses their first round of action. Unseen or silent parties add +2 to their surprise chance.

---

## Reaction Rolls

Not every encounter is a fight. Roll 2d6 + CHA modifier of the party spokesperson when the outcome is uncertain.

| 2d6 + CHA | Reaction |
|-----------|----------|
| 2-3 | Hostile, attacks immediately |
| 4-5 | Unfriendly, hostile unless party acts carefully |
| 6-8 | Uncertain, waits and watches |
| 9-10 | Indifferent, not immediately hostile |
| 11-12 | Friendly, open to interaction |

Apply reaction rolls to all encounters including monsters.

---

## Morale

Check morale when monsters take significant losses or face overwhelming odds. Roll 2d6 vs morale score. Fail = flee or surrender.

| Morale | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| 6 | Cowardly: goblins, hired thugs |
| 8 | Average: orcs, guards |
| 10 | Determined: veterans, fanatics |
| 12 | Unbreakable: constructs, undead, bound spirits |

---

## The Die of Fate in Encounters

When the answer to a situational question depends on circumstance rather than character capability, roll d6: 1-3 unfavorable, 4-6 favorable. Adjust the range for context. Apply the relevant character's LUC modifier.

*Is the guard asleep? Did the patrol double back? Is there another way out?*

See [Non-combat Actions](../game-rules/noncombat-actions.md) for full Die of Fate rules.
