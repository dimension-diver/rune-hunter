# Downtime

Downtime is what happens in town between expeditions. It costs treasure to maintain yourself, and it is the primary venue for healing, advancement, and preparation.

**Upkeep:** 1 treasure per character per week. A party of 4 burns 4 treasure per week. This covers lodging, food, and basic resupply.

---

## Downtime Activities

Each week of downtime a character may pursue one activity.

| Activity | Cost | Time | Result |
|----------|------|------|--------|
| Rest and recover | 1 treasure | 1 week | Full HP restoration |
| Injury treatment | 1 treasure/slot | 1 week | Clear one injury condition |
| Spell research | 2 treasure | 1 week | 2-in-6 to learn new spell (Tier 3+: 1-in-6) |
| Carousing | 1 treasure | 1 week | 1d3 LUC restored; possible complications |
| Training | 2 treasure | 1 week | Practice specific skill or technique |
| Crafting | varies | 1-4 weeks | Produce equipment, gadgets, or scrolls |
| Networking | 1 treasure | 1 week | Establish or deepen a contact |
| Hireling recruitment | 1 treasure | 1 week | Roll to find available hirelings |

---

## Specialist Services

Specialists are available in towns and cities. Finding them may require networking or reputation.

| Service | Cost | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Injury treatment (per slot) | 1 treasure | Healer or surgeon |
| Disease treatment (per slot) | 1 treasure | Herbalist or alchemist |
| Curse/hex removal (per slot) | 1-3 treasure | Temple or priest |
| Jinx removal | varies | Requires narrative resolution; not just treasure |
| NPC spellcasting (per tier) | 1-2 treasure | Rare outside cities |
| Relic tech repair | 2-5 treasure | Requires technician; parts extra |

---

## Hiring Followers

Recruitment is a downtime activity. Roll 2d6 + CHA after spending 1 treasure and 1 week.

| Roll | Result |
|------|--------|
| 2-4 | No takers |
| 5-7 | 1d3 common hirelings |
| 8-10 | 2d3 common or 1 skilled |
| 11-12 | Up to 3 skilled hirelings |
| 13+ | A specialist seeks you out |

**Retainer recruitment:** requires reputation and CHA modifier 0+. Roll 2d6 + CHA vs 9. Success means a candidate approaches. Loyalty starts at 7.

---

## Carousing Complications

When carousing, roll 1d6 per treasure spent. On a 1: something went wrong. The referee rolls or chooses a complication: debt, enemies made, a secret revealed, a promise extracted, or a night with no memory at all.

---

## LUC Recovery

Carousing and downtime rest both restore LUC. Maximum recovery per week: 1d3 from carousing, up to starting score. LUC cannot be pushed above starting score through ordinary recovery.
