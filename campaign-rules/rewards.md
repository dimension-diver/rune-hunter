# Rewards & Accomplishments

## Expedition Rewards

At the end of each expedition with a significant objective completed, award one of the following. The reward should connect to what actually happened.

| Reward | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| +1 to a d6 task | Specific task or category |
| +1 to a stat | Referee determines or player makes a case |
| +1 to a save | Permanent; tied to what was survived |
| New talent | Requires fictional justification |

---

## Accomplishments

An Accomplishment is a deed of sufficient magnitude to mark a transition in who the character is. It unlocks the next level band. The reward is always a choice: advance to the next band, or take a concrete bonus (+1 to a stat, +1 to all saves of a type, or a significant talent) and stay at current tier.

**Advancing is always optional.** This is a commitment. The world will treat the character differently at higher tiers. A character may refuse the advance indefinitely.

---

## Calibrating Accomplishments

The following examples illustrate what qualifies at each transition. They are not requirements; the referee judges the table's fiction.

**Adventurer to Hero (levels 1-2 to 3-4)**
- Recover a significant relic and return it to a community that needs it
- Defeat a threat that has terrorized a region
- Establish a lasting alliance between two factions
- Return from a site others entered and did not

**Hero to Lord (levels 3-4 to 5-6)**
- Claim a seat of power and hold it
- Overthrow a regional power and choose what rises in its place
- Complete a quest of legendary difficulty
- Earn the permanent service of a powerful NPC, spirit, or faction

**Lord to Legend (levels 5-6 to 7-8)**
- Conquer or liberate a city; reshape a nation
- Destroy or seal something that has threatened the world for generations
- Found an order, institution, or dynasty that will outlast you
- Make a bargain with or defeat a being of mythic power

**Legend to Mythic (levels 7-8 to 9-10)**
- Reshape the political order of a continent
- Defeat a truly ancient threat
- Achieve something that changes the nature of the world itself
