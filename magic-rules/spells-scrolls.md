# Spells & Scrolls

## Traditions

Magic comes in two traditions, differing in source, governing stat, and what the caster can do outside of combat.

**Mage (Rune Magic):** WIS-based. Spells are learned from books, inscriptions, and teachers. Casters and Seekers follow this tradition. Can read scrolls, scribe scrolls, and craft magic items.

**Shaman (Spirit Magic):** CHA-based. Spells are learned by communing with spirits and negotiating access to their power. Espers follow this tradition. Cannot use scrolls or craft magic items; their relationship with magic is personal and relational, not transferable.

**Artificer:** A third approach. Artificers store prepared spells in gadgets. Anyone can activate a charged gadget; the activator pays no slot cost. Gadgets cannot be swapped mid-expedition. No casting time or interruption risk.

---

## Preparing Spells

Casters prepare spells from a spellbook after a full rest, filling their available spell slots. Espers prepare spirit invocations through communion rather than study. Prepared spells are expended when cast.

**Spells known** are capped by WIS score. A Caster with WIS 12 may know up to 12 spells total across all tiers.

---

## Casting

**Casting time:** Tier 1 spells resolve immediately. Higher tiers take a number of rounds equal to their tier. A caster who takes damage while casting loses the spell and the slot.

**Armor penalty:** wearing armor while casting adds the armor's AR value to enemy saves against the spell.

---

## Spell Research

Spell research has two uses:

**Learning a new rune or spirit invocation:** requires a source -- an inscription, a teacher, or a spirit willing to teach. Roll 2-in-6 after 1 week and 2 treasure. Higher tiers are harder: treat as 1-in-6 for Tier 3+.

**Learning a new invocation of a known rune:** the primary advancement path for Casters. A known rune can be invoked in qualitatively different ways at higher tiers. BOLT at 1 MP hits one target; at 3 MP arcs to multiple targets; at 5 MP becomes a zone discharge. Higher invocations must be discovered or researched.

---

## Scrolls

Scrolls contain a single spell of any tier. Activating a scroll does not require a spell slot but does require the ability to read them.

Delvers and Nobles can read scrolls. Casters and Seekers can read and scribe scrolls. Espers and Shamans cannot use scrolls.

Scribing a scroll costs 1 week and 1-3 treasure depending on tier. Scrolls are single use.
