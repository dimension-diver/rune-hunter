# Wounds & Recovery

## Defeat

At 0 HP a character is **defeated**: not automatically dead. The referee determines consequences based on the fiction -- captured, left for dead, grievously wounded. Death is a possible outcome, not the automatic one.

---

## Conditions

Conditions occupy inventory slots until removed. A character with all slots filled by conditions is incapacitated.

| Type | Removed by | Cost |
|------|-----------|------|
| Injury | Healer, surgeon, or rest | 1 treasure/slot |
| Disease | Herbalist or antidote | 1 treasure/slot |
| Curse / hex | Temple or ritual | 1-3 treasure/slot |
| Jinx | Spirit appeasement or quest | Narrative |
| Magical binding | Specific ritual | Narrative |

| Severity | Slots | Examples |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Minor | 1 | Sprained ankle, mild fever |
| Moderate | 2 | Broken bone, disease |
| Severe | 3 | Crippling wound, wasting sickness |
| Catastrophic | 4+ | Lost limb, death curse |

**Ability damage:** injury can deal 1d6 STR damage; poison deals 1d6 RES (save or repeat daily); Jinx deals 1d6 LUC. 0 in any stat has severe consequences. 0 RES = death.

---

## Healing

**In the field:** a healer's kit and a full dungeon turn (10 minutes, no threats) allows treatment of 1 minor injury condition.

**Rest:** a full dungeon turn of rest restores 1d4 + RES modifier HP (minimum 0).

**Full rest:** a night's sleep with no significant threats restores HP to maximum. Conditions are not cleared by rest alone.

**Specialist treatment:** see [Equipment & Treasure](../player-rules/equipment.md) for service costs. Full treatment procedures are covered in [Downtime](../campaign-rules/downtime.md).

---

## Armor Degradation

Critical hits (natural 20 on attack) reduce the target's AR by 1 for the duration of the fight. PC armor degraded this way requires downtime repair. Magic armor repairs itself between expeditions.
