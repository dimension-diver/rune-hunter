# Rune Hunter — Kith & Chimera

---

## What You Are

Your genos is what you look like and where you come from. It gives you one mechanical trait. Everything else, how people react to you, what you can attempt, what environments suit you, is handled by the referee applying +1 or -1 when it is genuinely relevant.

There are no hard boundaries between types. Two communities that call themselves the same thing may look and live completely differently. Two communities that look similar may have no connection at all. The ancient world engineered endlessly. What grew back from that engineering is still growing.

Tell the referee what you are. They will work with you.

---

## Kith

Kith are the human analogs. They vary enormously in color, feature, and build, but to most eyes they read as people. They are probably descended from baseline humans, shaped over centuries by environment, culture, and whatever the ancients were doing before everything collapsed. Think Hylians, Alteans, Gelflings, the various peoples of Morrowind. Clearly us. Clearly not quite.

Kith have no single physical trait. Their advantage is legibility: most institutions, most social systems, most assumptions in the world were built around people who look like Kith. They face fewer friction points in civilization.

**Trait:** Adaptability. Once per session reroll any one die roll that affects you. Take the new result.

---

## Chimera

Chimera are the beast-people. Every kind of human-animal combination you can imagine probably exists somewhere. The Chimera bucket covers everything from the cat-people of deep forests to sea-folk to subterranean diggers to beings that have barely been seen outside their home territory. Think the beast clans of Breath of Fire, the Beastmen of Elder Scrolls, the communities of She-Ra.

Each Chimera type has one mechanical trait reflecting their physical nature. Everything else is fiction.

---

## Named Genos

These are the groups the referee foregrounds. They are examples, not a complete list.

---

### Ctarl
*Kith-adjacent Chimera. Anime cat people. Forest dwelling. The closest thing to an elf analog.*

Graceful, long-lived, and at home in the deep forest. Ctarl look more Kith than most Chimera, which gets them into places other beast-people cannot go. Their Casters are almost always Shamans. Their scholars are drawn to the old archives. Think Fam from Ruin Explorers. Think Deedlit.

**Trait:** Safe landing. Never take fall damage regardless of height. Always land on their feet. Climbing is treated as having a relevant background warrant.

*Ctarl with claws deal d4 unarmed damage. This is not a trait, it is just true.*

---

### Kobold
*Subterranean mole and rat people. The closest thing to a dwarf analog. Not the DnD version.*

Kobolds dig. Their communities are underground, their architecture is labyrinthine, and their sense of spatial orientation is preternatural. They are stocky, short-sighted above ground, and extremely good at finding structural weaknesses. They have been delving ancient ruins longer than anyone else.

**Trait:** Underground sense. Always know direction and approximate depth underground. +1 to d6 rolls involving stonework, tunnels, structural assessment, or underground navigation.

---

### Goblin
*Subterranean bat people. Not the DnD version.*

Goblins navigate by sound. They are rarely seen above ground by choice, though some communities have adapted to surface life over generations. Their echolocation is not magic, it is biology, and it works in complete darkness. They are smaller than Kobolds, faster, and considerably less patient.

**Trait:** Echolocation. Can navigate and detect moving creatures in complete darkness within 30ft. Loud or chaotic noise interferes with this.

---

### Zora
*Aquatic fish and sea people. Mermista's people.*

Zora communities exist wherever there is deep enough water. Coastal Zora and river Zora may look and live very differently. Some are fully aquatic, some amphibious. All are at home in water in a way no other genos matches.

**Trait:** Aquatic. Breathe and move freely underwater at full speed. No penalties in any aquatic environment.

---

### Rito
*Bird people. They can fly.*

Rito are light-boned and hollow-lunged. They can fly, which is an enormous advantage, bounded by one hard constraint: flight requires being unburdened. They are also found in high places: mountain settlements, coastal cliffs, tall ruins.

**Trait:** Flight. Can fly at normal movement speed while carrying 4 or fewer filled inventory slots. Each slot above 4 reduces flight to gliding only. Above 6 slots, cannot fly.

---

### Kreen
*Bug people. Survivalist pack culture. Cast out individuals become Pariah.*

Kreen live in small tight-knit packs in inhospitable territory: deep desert, blighted land, places where nothing else survives. They communicate partly through scent and vibration. Their chitin is natural armor. To most of the civilized world they are completely alien.

**Trait:** Natural chitin. AR 1 with no armor slot required. Cannot be surprised. Keen senses (smell and vibration) give +1 to detect hidden creatures or ambushes.

*See also: Pariah background (roll 12 on backgrounds table).*

---

### Ronso
*Bestial cat people. Khajit meets Hrothgar. Lives in hard places.*

Ronso are big. Desert Ronso and tundra Ronso may look quite different but share a physical culture built around endurance and direct confrontation. Some have a single horn or pronounced brow ridge. All of them are hard to stop once moving.

**Trait:** Powerful build. Can carry 2 additional inventory slots without encumbrance penalty. Once per encounter, move through an enemy's zone without triggering a free attack.

---

### Ygguana
*(Also called Yggonians or Marsh Lizards. Argonian analog.)*

The Ygguana were created, not evolved, by something called the Ygg: a vast slow alien organism that predates the civilization the Rune Hunters are delving through. The Ygg still exists in the deep marshes and ancient forests. Ygguana with strong connections to their home Ygg carry a faint psychic resonance with the land around it. This is not magic. It is just what they are.

**Trait:** Aquatic endurance. Breathe and move freely underwater at full speed. No penalties in any aquatic environment. Can hold breath for an extraordinary duration above water as well.

*The Ygg-link is fluff, not a mechanic. The referee can invoke it when the fiction calls for it.*

---

### Ctarl
*Kith-adjacent Chimera. Anime cat people. Forest dwelling. The closest thing to an elf analog.*

Graceful, long-lived, and at home in the deep forest. Ctarl look more Kith than most Chimera, which gets them into places other beast-people cannot go. Their Casters are almost always Shamans. Their scholars are drawn to the old archives. Think Fam from Ruin Explorers. Think Deedlit.

**Trait:** Safe landing. Always take minimum possible fall damage (1 per die rather than rolling). Always land on their feet. Climbing is treated as having a relevant background warrant.

*Ctarl with claws deal d4 unarmed damage. This is not a trait, it is just true.*

---

### Wolf People
*(Placeholder name. Details and specific culture TBD.)*

Pack-oriented, endurance hunters, and absolutely impossible to sneak up on. Wolf people communities vary enormously in appearance and culture depending on their territory, but the nose is always the constant. They smell you coming. They smell what you had for breakfast. They smell that you are afraid.

**Trait:** Keen scent. Cannot be surprised by anything with a scent, unless that scent is specifically masked. +1 to tracking and detection tasks involving smell. In the dark or fog, can locate hidden creatures by scent alone within 30ft.

*Wolf people with a bite deal d4 unarmed damage. This is not a trait, it is just true.*

## Design Note

Every character gets all three layers: genos trait, background talent, and type. These stack. A Ctarl Shaman Hunter has safe landing, spirit sense, and FC progression. Nothing is replaced or excluded.

One trait per genos. That is all. The rest is fiction, context, and the referee applying +1 or -1 when it genuinely matters.

**Natural weapons:** if your genos has claws, a bite, or similar natural weapons, you deal d4 unarmed damage instead of 1. This is not a trait. It is just what is true about you. The referee should apply this as common sense whenever relevant.

A Rito trying to fly through a cave system faces obvious problems. A Zora negotiating in a desert city faces social friction. A Kreen walking into a civilized settlement reads differently than a Kith doing the same thing. None of this requires a rule. It requires a referee paying attention to the fiction.

The named genos above are the ones the setting foregrounds. There are more. Many more. If a player wants to play something not on this list, work it out: describe what they are, identify one physical or cultural trait that has mechanical weight, and proceed.
