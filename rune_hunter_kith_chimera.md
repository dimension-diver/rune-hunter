# Rune Hunter — Genos

Your genos is what you look like and where you come from. It gives you one or more mechanical traits. Everything else -- how people react to you, what environments suit you, what doors open or close -- is handled by the referee applying +1 or −1 when it genuinely matters.

The named genos below are the ones the setting foregrounds. There are more. If you want to play something not on this list, describe what you are, identify what has mechanical weight, and proceed.

**Natural weapons:** any genos with claws, a bite, or similar deals d4 unarmed damage. This is not a trait. It is just true.

---

**Kith** are the human analogs -- the Hylians, Alteans, Gelflings, the varied peoples of Morrowind. Clearly us. Clearly not quite. Most institutions, most social assumptions, most doors in the civilized world were built around people who look like Kith. Once per session, reroll any one die roll that affects you and take the new result.

**Ctarl** are cat-people: graceful, long-lived, at home in deep forest. They look more Kith than most Chimera, which gets them into places others cannot go. Their Casters are almost always Espers. Never take fall damage. Climbing tasks succeed on 1-3 in 6. Claws deal d4 unarmed damage.

**Kobold** are subterranean mole and rat people -- the closest thing to a dwarf analog, though nothing like the DnD version. They have been delving ancient ruins longer than anyone else. Always know direction and approximate depth underground. +1 to d6 rolls involving stonework, tunnels, or structural assessment.

**Goblin** are bat people who navigate by echolocation. Their sound-sense is biology, not magic, and works in complete darkness within 30ft. Rarely seen above ground by choice. Can navigate and detect moving creatures in darkness; loud noise interferes. +1 to initiative from sound awareness.

**Zora** are aquatic fish and sea people -- Mermista's people. Coastal and river Zora may look quite different but share the water. Breathe and move freely underwater at full speed. No penalties in any aquatic environment.

**Rito** are bird people. They can fly. Light-boned and hollow-lunged, they are found in high places: mountain settlements, coastal cliffs, tall ruins. Can fly at normal movement speed while carrying 4 or fewer inventory slots. Each slot above 4 reduces flight to gliding. Above 6 slots, no flight.

**Kreen** are insectoid people who live in small tight-knit packs in inhospitable territory -- deep desert, blighted land, places nothing else survives. Their chitin is natural armor. AR 1 with no armor slot required. Cannot be surprised. +1 to detect hidden creatures or ambushes by scent and vibration.

**Ronso** are large bestial cat people built for endurance and direct confrontation -- Khajit meets Hrothgar. Some have a horn or heavy brow ridge. Carry 2 additional inventory slots without encumbrance penalty. Once per encounter, move through an enemy zone without triggering a free attack.

**Ygguana** are lizard people created by the Ygg: a vast, slow alien organism that predates the civilization being delved. The Ygg still exists in deep marshes and ancient forests. Ygguana breathe and move freely underwater. They also hold their breath for extraordinary duration above water and resist poison -- +1 to saves against toxic effects.

**Wolf People** are pack hunters defined by an extraordinary sense of smell. They smell you coming. They smell what you had for breakfast. Cannot be surprised by anything with a scent unless specifically masked. +1 to tracking and detection tasks. Locate hidden creatures by scent alone within 30ft in darkness or fog. Bite deals d4 unarmed damage.

---

*The referee applies +1 or −1 whenever your genos is meaningfully relevant -- a Rito in a cave, a Zora in a desert, a Kreen walking into a civilized settlement. No rule needed. Just attention to fiction.*
